Business Problem

🏢 Company Context

Cyclistic is a Chicago-based bike-share company operating:

5,800+ bicycles across the city

600+ docking stations

2 rider types: Casual riders vs Annual Members

The Strategic Question

How can Cyclistic convert casual riders into annual members?

Annual members generate significantly more revenue than casual riders — making conversion the #1 growth lever for the marketing team.

🎯
Objective: Identify behavioural differences between rider types using April 2024 trip data and translate findings into a data-driven marketing strategy that can convert casual riders into annual members.

03

Key Insights

⏱️

Ride Duration Gap

21 min (casual) vs 12 min (member)

Casual riders take nearly 2× longer per trip. Classic bike casual avg: 41 min — a clear signal of leisure and recreational use, not commuting.

📅

Weekend Concentration

43% of casual rides on weekends

43% of casual rides fall on Saturday–Sunday vs only 25% for members. Casual riders are fundamentally weekend-first users.

🕐

Commuter vs Leisure Pattern

8AM & 5PM vs 2–5PM peak

Members spike at 8AM and 5PM — unmistakable commute signature. Casual riders ramp gradually, peaking in the mid-afternoon.

📍

Leisure Station Hotspots

76–78% casual at top stations

Streeter Dr & Grand Ave and DuSable Lake Shore Dr are 76–78% casual — tourist & lakefront corridors, not commuter hubs.

04

Business Recommendations

4 Actions to Convert Casual → Member

01

Weekend Membership Campaign

Based on: 43% of casual rides on weekends
Launch a targeted Sat/Sun campaign offering a discounted first month of annual membership to casual riders with 3+ rides in a month.
Expected impact: Targets casual riders at their highest engagement moment — the start of a weekend ride.
02

Afternoon In-App Nudge

Based on: Casual peak 2–5PM on weekends
At 1:30PM on weekends, push a personalised cost comparison: "You've spent $X on day passes. A membership would save $Y."
Expected impact: Reaches casual riders 30 minutes before peak usage — when conversion intent is highest.
03

Long-Ride Loyalty Program

Based on: Casual rides avg 2× longer
After 5 rides exceeding 20 minutes in 30 days, trigger a one-click 20%-off membership upgrade offer.
Expected impact: Identifies the most conversion-ready casual users based on demonstrated behaviour.
04

Station-Level QR Marketing

Based on: 76–78% casual at top stations
Install membership sign-up QR codes at Streeter Dr, DuSable Lake Shore Dr, and Michigan Ave docks with a clear value prop.
Expected impact: Physical touchpoint at locations where casual-to-member conversion is most likely.
